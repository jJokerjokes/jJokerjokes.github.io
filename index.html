Project Goal¶

The goal of this project is to analyze air quality data from various regions and investigate its impact on public health, specifically focusing on respiratory diseases. Our primary objective is to correlate pollution levels with public health outcomes, such as hospital admissions and mortality rates. By using datasets like EPA Air Quality Data and the World Air Quality Index, we aim to forecast pollution trends, study the effects of pollutants on specific demographics (e.g., age groups), and visualize pollution levels geographically.

Table 1¶

This dataset, sourced from the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), provides detailed air quality information collected from monitoring stations across the United States for the year 2024. The dataset captures the concentration levels of various pollutants, including PM2.5 and ozone, measured hourly or over extended periods, depending on the pollutant. Each monitoring site is specified by its geographical location, along with associated metadata such as percentile readings, sampling duration, and local site details.

We chose this dataset for its relevance to studying the impacts of air pollution on public health. It enables us to explore correlations between pollution levels and health outcomes, such as respiratory illnesses or hospital admissions. The data's high resolution across time and location makes it ideal for identifying trends, analyzing seasonal variations in air quality, and evaluating the effectiveness of environmental policies.

Monthly Comparison of 50th and 75th Percentiles of PM2.5 Concentrations¶

This analysis compares the 50th percentile (median) and 75th percentile concentrations of PM2.5 across different months. The 50th percentile provides the median concentration, indicating typical PM2.5 levels, while the 75th percentile focuses on the higher end of the concentration distribution, highlighting periods with elevated pollution levels.

In [24]:
# Filter the dataset to only include rows where the 'Parameter Name' is PM2.5
pm25_df = df[df['Parameter Name'] == 'PM2.5 - Local Conditions']

# Group by month and calculate the mean for both 50th and 75th Percentiles for PM2.5
monthly_pm25_percentiles = pm25_df.groupby('Month')[['50th Percentile', '75th Percentile']].mean()

# Plotting both 50th (median) and 75th percentile trends for PM2.5
plt.figure(figsize=(8, 6))
plt.plot(monthly_pm25_percentiles.index, monthly_pm25_percentiles['50th Percentile'], marker='o', label='50th Percentile', color='g')
plt.plot(monthly_pm25_percentiles.index, monthly_pm25_percentiles['75th Percentile'], marker='o', label='75th Percentile', color='b')
plt.title('Comparison of 50th and 75th Percentiles of PM2.5 Concentration by Month', fontsize=14)
plt.xlabel('Month', fontsize=12)
plt.ylabel('PM2.5 Concentration', fontsize=12)
plt.xticks(monthly_pm25_percentiles.index)
plt.legend()
plt.grid(True)
plt.tight_layout()

plt.show()
